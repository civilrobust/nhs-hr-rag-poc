NHS TRUST ICT DEPARTMENT - DISCIPLINARY POLICY
Policy Reference: HR-ICT-004
Effective Date: January 2024

PURPOSE
This policy ensures fair and consistent handling of conduct and performance issues while maintaining service standards and Trust values.

SCOPE
Applies to all ICT staff for misconduct including:
- Unauthorized system access or data breaches
- Persistent lateness or absence
- Failure to follow IT security protocols
- Inappropriate use of Trust systems
- Insubordination or bullying

INFORMAL RESOLUTION
Minor issues should be resolved through informal discussion with line manager before formal proceedings.

FORMAL DISCIPLINARY PROCESS
Stage 1: Verbal Warning (remains on file 6 months)
Stage 2: Written Warning (remains on file 12 months)
Stage 3: Final Written Warning (remains on file 18 months)
Stage 4: Dismissal

INVESTIGATION
Allegations will be investigated before disciplinary action. Staff may be suspended on full pay during serious investigations.

RIGHT TO REPRESENTATION
Staff have the right to be accompanied by a trade union representative or workplace colleague at all formal meetings.

GROSS MISCONDUCT
Serious breaches may result in immediate dismissal without notice, including:
- Deliberate damage to Trust systems
- Theft or fraud
- Serious data protection breaches
- Violence or harassment
