NHS TRUST ICT DEPARTMENT - DATA PROTECTION & IT USAGE POLICY
Policy Reference: HR-ICT-006
Effective Date: January 2024

DATA PROTECTION PRINCIPLES
All ICT staff must handle personal data according to GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018:
- Process data lawfully, fairly, and transparently
- Collect data only for specified purposes
- Keep data accurate and up to date
- Retain data only as long as necessary
- Ensure appropriate security measures

ACCEPTABLE USE OF IT SYSTEMS
Trust IT systems may be used for:
- Work-related activities
- Reasonable personal use during breaks

Prohibited activities include:
- Accessing inappropriate or offensive content
- Installing unauthorized software
- Sharing login credentials
- Using personal devices for patient data
- Downloading unlicensed software

EMAIL AND INTERNET
Email is a business tool and should be professional. Internet access may be monitored for security purposes. Social media use during work hours should be minimal.

PASSWORD SECURITY
Staff must:
- Use strong passwords (minimum 12 characters)
- Change passwords every 90 days
- Never share passwords
- Use multi-factor authentication where available

INCIDENT REPORTING
Any data breach, security incident, or loss of equipment must be reported immediately to Information Security team and logged on Datix.

CONSEQUENCES
Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including dismissal. Serious breaches may be reported to ICO or police.
