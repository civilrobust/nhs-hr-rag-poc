NHS TRUST ICT DEPARTMENT - GRIEVANCE POLICY
Policy Reference: HR-ICT-005
Effective Date: January 2024

PURPOSE
This policy provides a framework for staff to raise concerns about work, working conditions, or relationships with colleagues.

INFORMAL RESOLUTION
Staff should first attempt to resolve issues informally by:
- Speaking directly with the person involved
- Discussing with line manager
- Seeking mediation support

FORMAL GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
If informal resolution fails, staff may submit a formal grievance in writing to their line manager including:
- Details of the complaint
- Relevant dates and incidents
- Desired outcome
- Any supporting evidence

GRIEVANCE MEETING
A formal meeting will be arranged within 10 working days where the employee can:
- Present their case
- Be accompanied by trade union representative or colleague
- Provide witness statements

INVESTIGATION
Line manager will investigate and provide a written response within 15 working days.

RIGHT TO APPEAL
If unsatisfied with the outcome, staff may appeal in writing to Head of ICT within 10 working days. Appeal decision is final.

SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES
Grievances about line manager should be submitted to next level manager. Concerns about discrimination or bullying have additional protections.
